# Voice & Style Guide

How to apply and enforce a brand voice profile. Covers tone flex
definitions, the enforcement procedure, style rules, and worked examples.

---

## Tone Flex Dimensions

Voice stays constant. Tone flexes along three dimensions:

| Dimension | What it controls | Low | Medium | High |
|-----------|-----------------|-----|--------|------|
| **Formality** | Language register | Casual, contractions, fragments | Professional but conversational | Structured, complete sentences |
| **Energy** | Urgency and dynamism | Measured, thoughtful | Balanced, informative | Active verbs, short sentences, momentum |
| **Technical Depth** | Domain expertise shown | Outcomes, benefits, no jargon | Terms explained in context | Technical terminology, specs, detail |

Energy also has a **Warm** level (between Low and Medium): empathetic,
supportive, understanding — for onboarding, sensitive topics, support
content.

## Enforcing the Profile

For every piece of content, check against the "We Are / We Are Not" table:

### Check "We Are" (presence)

1. Which 2-3 attributes are most relevant for this content type?
2. Does the content reflect them — not through specific words, but through
   the overall feel and approach?
3. If an attribute is missing, find a natural place to express it — don't
   force it into every paragraph.

### Check "We Are Not" (boundaries)

1. Does any passage cross a "We Are Not" boundary?
2. If so, revise that passage — the fix is usually subtle (soften one word,
   add a qualifier, shift the framing).
3. Crossing a boundary doesn't mean the whole piece is wrong. It means one
   moment went too far.

### Common Mistakes

1. **All attributes at maximum intensity.** Let 2-3 lead based on content
   type. A blog post teaching a concept doesn't need every attribute
   performing in every paragraph.

2. **Confusing voice with tone.** If a piece needs to be more casual,
   adjust TONE (lower formality, higher energy). Don't change VOICE — the
   brand's personality stays the same.

3. **Rigid enforcement over natural flow.** Guidelines are principles, not
   a checklist. Content should feel natural, not mechanically assembled
   from voice attributes.

4. **Ignoring the audience.** The same voice sounds different to a
   beginner vs. an expert. Tone flexes (especially Technical Depth)
   handle this.

5. **Treating "We Are Not" as banned vocabulary.** The column defines
   boundaries, not prohibited words. "We are not blunt" doesn't mean you
   can't be direct — it means directness should include empathy and context.

## Style Rules

### Grammar and Mechanics

Document and enforce these choices consistently:

| Rule | Options | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes / No | "fast, reliable, and secure" vs. "fast, reliable and secure" |
| Heading case | Sentence / Title | "How to get started" vs. "How to Get Started" |
| Contractions | Use / Avoid | "we're" vs. "we are" |
| Em dash spacing | No spaces / Spaces | "this—and more" vs. "this — and more" |
| Numbers | Spell out 1-9 / Always numerals | "five features" vs. "5 features" |
| Percent | % / percent | "50%" vs. "50 percent" |
| Date format | Month DD, YYYY / DD/MM/YYYY | "January 15, 2025" |
| Lists | Periods on full sentences / No periods on fragments | Context-dependent |

### Formatting Conventions

- Heading hierarchy (when to use H1, H2, H3)
- Bold and italic usage (bold for emphasis, italic for titles/terms)
- Link text (always descriptive, never "click here")
- Code formatting (for technical brands)

### Punctuation and Emphasis

- Exclamation marks: limited use, never more than one
- Ellipsis: avoid in most professional contexts
- ALL CAPS: avoid; use bold for emphasis instead
- Emoji: by channel (professional = minimal, social = where appropriate)

### Inclusive Language

- Use gender-neutral language (they/them for unknown individuals)
- Avoid ableist language ("crazy," "blind spot," "lame")
- Use "simple" or "straightforward" instead of "easy"
- Avoid culturally specific idioms that may not translate

## Before/After Examples

These use a fictional brand to demonstrate enforcement:

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| **Practical** — specific, actionable advice | **Vague** — no generalities or hand-waving |
| **Direct** — get to the point, respect time | **Blunt** — directness includes empathy |
| **Experienced** — we've done this work | **Gatekeeping** — experience shared, not hoarded |
| **Honest** — admit complexity and tradeoffs | **Cynical** — honesty isn't negativity |

Tone for Blog/SEO: Formality Medium, Energy Medium, Technical Depth
Medium-High.

### Blog Intro

**Before (generic):**

> In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, search engine optimization
> has become an essential component of any successful marketing strategy.
> Understanding how to effectively optimize your content can make the
> difference between online visibility and obscurity. This comprehensive
> guide will walk you through everything you need to know about on-page SEO
> in 2026.

**After (voice enforced):**

> Most on-page SEO advice tells you to "optimize your headers" and "use
> keywords naturally" without showing you what that actually looks like on a
> live page. We're going to fix that. This guide breaks down the 7 on-page
> signals that moved the needle on 40+ pages we optimized last quarter —
> with the exact changes we made and the traffic impact of each.

**What changed:** Practical (promises specific advice), Direct (no
throat-clearing), Experienced (references real work), Honest (acknowledges
the problem). "Before" crosses Vague — "essential component," "everything
you need to know."

### Explanatory Section

**Before (generic):**

> Internal linking is a crucial aspect of SEO that many website owners
> overlook. By strategically linking between pages on your site, you can
> help search engines understand your site structure and distribute page
> authority more effectively.

**After (voice enforced):**

> Internal links do two things: they tell Google which pages matter, and
> they keep readers moving deeper into your site instead of bouncing. Most
> sites have the links — they're just pointing in the wrong direction.
>
> Here's the pattern we see constantly: blog posts link to the homepage and
> the about page. Neither needs more authority. The pages that need link
> equity are your service pages, comparison posts, and anything targeting
> competitive keywords.
>
> The fix takes 5 minutes per post: find your top-traffic blog posts (check
> Google Search Console → Pages), then add 2-3 links from each to the pages
> you actually want to rank.

**What changed:** Practical (exact steps, where to find data), Direct
(straight to function), Experienced ("pattern we see constantly"), Honest
(doesn't pretend the reader has zero links). "Before" crosses Vague —
"crucial aspect," "more effectively."
