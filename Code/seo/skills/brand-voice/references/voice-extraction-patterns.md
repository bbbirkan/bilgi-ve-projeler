# Voice Extraction Patterns

Detailed heuristics and scoring rubrics for extracting brand voice from
content across the 6 dimensions.

---

## Corpus Requirements

- **Minimum:** 3 pieces of content, 500+ words total
- **Ideal:** 5-8 pieces across multiple formats, 2,000+ words total
- **Best results:** Content from different channels (website, email,
  social) that the brand considers representative
- **Red flag:** If all content sounds different, the brand may not have a
  defined voice yet — consider Build mode

---

## Dimension 1: Tone Spectrum Scoring

Place the brand on each axis based on observed patterns:

### Formal ↔ Casual
| Signal | Formal | Casual |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Contractions | Rarely/never | Frequently |
| Sentence fragments | None | Common |
| Slang/colloquialisms | Absent | Present |
| Greetings | "Dear..." / none | "Hey" / "Hi there" |
| Sign-offs | "Sincerely" / "Regards" | "Cheers" / first name |

### Serious ↔ Playful
| Signal | Serious | Playful |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Humor | Absent or very dry | Present, sometimes the point |
| Metaphors | Conventional | Creative/unexpected |
| Pop culture references | Rare | Common |
| Emoji | Never | Used as punctuation |
| Self-deprecation | Absent | Present |

### Reserved ↔ Bold
| Signal | Reserved | Bold |
|--------|----------|------|
| Hedging language | "might," "could," "perhaps" | Absent |
| Claims | Qualified, careful | Direct, specific |
| Opinions | Balanced, both-sides | Strong, definitive |
| Numbers | Ranges, approximations | Exact, specific |
| Confidence markers | "I think," "in my experience" | Stated as fact |

### Simple ↔ Sophisticated
| Signal | Simple | Sophisticated |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Word choice | Everyday words | Technical/academic vocabulary |
| Sentence structure | Short, direct | Complex, layered |
| Concept handling | One idea per sentence | Multiple ideas, nuance |
| Audience assumption | Explains everything | Expects prior knowledge |

### Warm ↔ Direct
| Signal | Warm | Direct |
|--------|------|--------|
| Reader acknowledgment | Empathetic, validating | Minimal |
| Transitional warmth | "Here's the thing..." | Gets to the point |
| Personal stories | Frequent | Rare |
| Emotional language | Present | Factual/neutral |

## Dimension 2: Vocabulary Fingerprinting

### Detection method
1. **Frequency scan:** Identify words/phrases that appear 3+ times across
   content pieces (signature vocabulary)
2. **Absence scan:** Note common industry words that are conspicuously
   absent (intentional avoidance)
3. **Jargon audit:** Categorize technical terms as:
   - Used and unexplained (audience assumed to know)
   - Used and explained (translating for audience)
   - Avoided entirely (simplified for accessibility)
4. **Register check:** Compare against plain-language alternatives. Does
   the brand say "utilize" or "use"? "Comprehensive" or "complete"?
5. **Profanity/edge scan:** Note any strong language, slang, or
   deliberately provocative word choices

## Dimension 3: Rhythm Analysis

### Sentence length distribution
- Count sentence lengths across a representative sample
- **Short-dominant:** avg 5-10 words, frequent fragments
- **Medium-balanced:** avg 12-18 words, mix of short and medium
- **Long-flowing:** avg 20+ words, complex structure

### Paragraph patterns
- **Tight:** 1-2 sentences per paragraph, lots of white space
- **Standard:** 3-4 sentences, conventional structure
- **Dense:** 5+ sentences, academic/editorial style

### Rhythm signature
- Does the brand alternate short/long deliberately? (punchy-punchy-long)
- Do they use one-word or one-line paragraphs for emphasis?
- Is there a predictable cadence or is it varied?

## Dimension 4: Structural Pattern Detection

### Opening patterns
| Pattern | Example signal |
|---------|---------------|
| Bold statement | "Most advice about X is wrong." |
| Question | "Ever wonder why X doesn't work?" |
| Story/anecdote | "Last Tuesday, I..." |
| Data point | "73% of marketers..." |
| Personal intro | "Hey, it's [Name]" |
| Reframe | "Everyone talks about X. The real issue is Y." |

### Transition patterns
- Smooth connectors ("That said," "Here's where it gets interesting")
- Abrupt shifts (new header, no transition)
- Conversational ("OK so here's the thing")
- Numbered/sequential ("First... Second... Third...")

### Closing patterns
| Pattern | Signal |
|---------|--------|
| Direct CTA | "Sign up now" / "Try it free" |
| Soft CTA | "If this resonated, you might also like..." |
| Summary | Restates the key takeaway |
| Open loop | Teases what's next without resolving |
| Personal sign-off | "Talk soon" / name signature |

## Dimension 5: Personality Signal Indicators

### Confidence mapping
- **Self-deprecating:** "I screwed this up for years before..."
- **Humble-confident:** "Here's what I've found works..."
- **Authoritative:** "The data is clear..." / "This is how it works."
- **Aggressive-confident:** "Anyone who tells you otherwise is wrong."

### Stance detection
| Stance | Signals |
|--------|---------|
| Teacher | Explains concepts, uses "let me show you," structured lessons |
| Peer | "We're all figuring this out," shares failures, equal footing |
| Rebel | Challenges conventions, "here's what nobody tells you" |
| Guide | "I've been where you are," leads from experience, maps the path |
| Insider | "Here's what actually happens," behind-the-scenes access |

### Polish level
- **Raw:** Typos tolerated, stream-of-consciousness feel, "thinking out loud"
- **Conversational:** Clean but informal, reads like good spoken language
- **Polished:** Careful word choice, no loose ends, editorial quality
- **Formal:** Corporate precision, reviewed and approved feel

---

## Dimension 6: POV Consistency

### First person check
- **I-dominant:** Personal brand, individual voice
- **We-dominant:** Company voice, team identity
- **Mixed:** Switches between I/we (check if intentional or inconsistent)
- **Absent:** Third person or instructional ("do this") without personal pronouns

### Reader address
- **Direct "you":** Most common, creates connection
- **Inclusive "we":** "We all know..." / "Let's look at..."
- **Named groups:** "Founders," "marketers," "builders"
- **Informal address:** "Folks," "friends," "y'all"

### Consistency check
If POV shifts between content pieces, note whether:
- It's channel-appropriate (LinkedIn = "we," Twitter = "I")
- Or inconsistent (same channel, different POV = voice not defined yet)

---

## Cross-Platform Consistency Scoring

When content exists across multiple channels, score consistency:

- **High consistency:** Same personality recognizable everywhere, only tone
  shifts by channel (this is ideal)
- **Moderate consistency:** Core voice present but some channels feel
  different (common — the profile should codify the core and note channel
  adaptations)
- **Low consistency:** Different channels sound like different brands
  (the profile needs to establish a baseline first, then define how it
  adapts)
