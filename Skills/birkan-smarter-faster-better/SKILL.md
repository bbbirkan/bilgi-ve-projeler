# Smarter Faster Better: The Power of Habit and Productivity

## Overview

*Smarter Faster Better* by Charles Duhigg is a comprehensive exploration of productivity across individuals and organizations. Based on neuroscience, psychology, and real-world case studies, this book reveals the eight core principles that distinguish genuinely productive people and companies from those merely going through the motions.

**Key Principle**: Productivity isn't about working more or harder—it's about making certain choices in certain ways. It's the science of making better decisions and building cultures of excellence.

## 📖 Complete Chapter Structure

### 1. **MOTIVATION**
*Reimagining Boot Camp, Nursing Home Rebellions, and the Locus of Control*

How a feeling of control generates motivation. The military transforms directionless teenagers into marines by teaching them choices "biased toward action." Learn how to reframe constraints as opportunities and boost your sense of agency.

**Key Concepts**: Locus of control, psychological reactance, self-efficacy, motivation through autonomy

### 2. **TEAMS**
*Psychological Safety at Google and Saturday Night Live*

Why some teams excel while others fail. Google's research (Project Aristotle) and SNL's creative success both point to psychological safety—the belief that you can take interpersonal risks without fear of negative consequences.

**Key Concepts**: Psychological safety, team dynamics, risk-taking, trust, group norms

### 3. **FOCUS**
*Cognitive Tunneling, Air France Flight 447, and the Power of Mental Models*

How pilots prevent catastrophe by telling themselves stories. Using mental models to maintain focus despite overwhelming information. Learn why cognitive tunneling happens and how to counteract it.

**Key Concepts**: Mental models, cognitive tunneling, attention, narrative construction, situational awareness

### 4. **GOAL SETTING**
*Smart Goals, Stretch Goals, and the Yom Kippur War*

The balance between incremental SMART goals and ambitious stretch goals. Israel's failure in the Yom Kippur War shows what happens when leaders fixate on the wrong aspirations. Discover the framework for setting goals that drive results.

**Key Concepts**: SMART goals, stretch goals, goal alignment, ambition, performance management

### 5. **MANAGING OTHERS**
*Solving a Kidnapping with Lean and Agile Thinking and a Culture of Trust*

FBI agents solved a kidnapping through agile management principles. Lean thinking and a culture of trust transform how organizations respond to crises. Learn to build "commitment cultures" that support employees even when commitment gets hard.

**Key Concepts**: Agile methodology, lean thinking, commitment culture, empowerment, crisis management

### 6. **DECISION MAKING**
*Forecasting the Future (and Winning at Poker) with Bayesian Psychology*

How to envision the future as multiple possibilities rather than fixating on what you hope will happen. A woman won a national poker championship using probabilistic thinking. Understand cognitive biases and how Bayesian reasoning improves decisions.

**Key Concepts**: Bayesian thinking, probabilistic reasoning, cognitive bias, forecasting, scenario planning

### 7. **INNOVATION**
*How Idea Brokers and Creative Desperation Saved Disney's Frozen*

Disney's creative teams nearly canceled Frozen. Creative tension, diversity of thought, and idea brokerage saved it. Learn how combining disparate ideas creates breakthrough innovation and how to foster creative cultures.

**Key Concepts**: Idea brokerage, creative tension, diversity, innovation frameworks, organizational culture

### 8. **ABSORBING DATA**
*Turning Information into Knowledge in Cincinnati's Public Schools*

How Cincinnati transformed education by making information harder to absorb, paradoxically improving learning. Understanding cognitive load theory and information design. Data literacy and knowledge management in organizations.

**Key Concepts**: Information design, cognitive load, knowledge management, data literacy, learning science

### The Eight Principles of Productivity

1. **Motivation** comes from perceived control and the ability to make choices
2. **Teams** succeed when psychological safety enables risk-taking and trust
3. **Focus** is maintained through mental models and narrative construction
4. **Goals** require balance between specific targets (SMART) and ambitious vision (stretch)
5. **Management** succeeds through agile, lean approaches and cultures of trust
6. **Decisions** improve when using probabilistic thinking instead of hope-based reasoning
7. **Innovation** emerges from creative tension, diversity, and idea brokerage
8. **Information** becomes knowledge through thoughtful design and cognitive understanding

## 💭 Core Thesis

The way we choose to see ourselves and frame daily decisions; the stories we tell ourselves, and the easy goals we ignore; the sense of community we build among teammates; the creative cultures we establish as leaders: These are the things that separate the merely busy from the genuinely productive."
